# Global-superstore-Sales-Revenue-Analysis
Excel analysis of Global Superstore sales &amp; revenue (2011-2014). Covers $12M revenue, $1.37M profit, 169K units sold across 7 markets. Includes trends, customer segments, product categories, and profitability insights.

## Project Overview
This project presents a comprehensive analysis of Global Superstore's sales and revenue data from 2011 to 2014. Using Excel for data manipulation, analysis, and visualization, this project addresses key business questions to provide actionable insights for strategic decision-making.

## Key Metrics Summary
- **Total Revenue**: $12,004,962.05
- **Total Profit**: $1,372,521.96
- **Total Quantity Sold**: 169,620 units
- **Total Cost**: $10,632,440.09
- **Time Period**: 2011-2014
- **Markets Analyzed**: 7 (APAC, US, LATAM, EU, EMEA, Africa, Canada)

### Market Performance
- **US market leads** in total order quantity (37,873 units)
- **APAC and US** are the top performing markets
- **EU and LATAM** show strong performance with 35K+ units each
- **Africa and Canada** represent smaller but growing markets

### Profitability Analysis
- Products are categorized into **4 profitability segments**:
  - High profitability: 6,943 orders
  - Medium profitability: 22,120 orders
  - Low profitability: 6,850 orders
  - Non-profitable: 13,134 orders
- **Over 26% of orders are non-profitable**, indicating need for pricing strategy review

### Temporal Trends
- **2014 showed the highest profit** at $470,411.20
- **Steady growth** from 2011 ($232,730.25) to 2014
- **Order volume increased** from 8,600 (2011) to 16,824 (2014)
- **September peak**: Highest monthly profit at $157,343.92
- **June also shows strong performance** with $136,542.38 in profit

### Product & Customer Insights
- Analysis spans multiple product categories and sub-categories
- Customer segmentation reveals distinct purchasing patterns
- Shipping modes impact both cost and customer satisfaction

## Strategic Recommendations
1. **Focus on profitability**: Address the 26% non-profitable orders
2. **Seasonal planning**: Capitalize on Q3 peaks (September)
3. **Market expansion**: Leverage success in US and APAC for other regions
4. **Product optimization**: Prioritize high-margin products
5. **Return reduction**: Implement quality control measures
